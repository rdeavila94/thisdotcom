Hi, I'm   Richard

... and welcome to my residence on the world wide web! Like most Los Angeles homes, it's small and compact.
As you may have guessed, I reside in the L.A. area, where I was both born and raised. I like my avocado, beaches, , amusement parks, and Winter-time snow-capped mountains. I speak two languages and code in many more.
My introduction to coding occurred during my undergrad, where I received my B.S. in Computer Science and a minor in Web Technology at California State University, Long Beach. The curriculum consisted of standard Computer Science coursework whilst my minor added a specialization in web development, beginning with HTML, CSS, & JavaScript and ending with LAMP stack development.
Besides the technical mumbo jumbo, my life involves a plethora of other interests and hobbies, which I believe is fundamental for keeping the mind invigorated. Like most city folk, I'm constantly active and up to something, so if I'm not answering the phone the likely culprit is: , (sorry, can't pause it), , , , or . I could also be busy with strength day at the gym, entranced by some dank memes, or plugged out of the world and listening to my favorite podcasts.

I specialize in web application development with Java and JavaScript.
I use a typical enterprise Java development stack involving: Maven, Spring, Hibernate, Oracle (or any major database, depending on how the data access layer is abstracted), and Thymeleaf (or JSP, if necessary).
Full-stack development with JavaScript is accomplished — obviously — with Node.js, particularly using the wittily dubbed "MEVN" stack, in contrast to the MEAN stack. For front-end development my orientation is towards Vue out of the many JS frameworks although Vanilla JS, the framework that never was, is held dear to my heart. Regarding styling, I have an affinity towards Sass and prefer it over plain CSS. Of course, I also have Bootstrap in my toolkit.
Outside of the aforementioned tech, I also like to dabble in functional programming with Elixir and desktop development with Electron.
